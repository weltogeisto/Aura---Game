## THE HIDDEN BOOK: Max Stirner

### Physical Appearance
- Faded ochre spine
- German text: *Der Einzige und sein Eigenthum*
- Tucked sideways behind Feuerbach's *Essence of Christianity* and Hegel's *Phenomenology*
- Requires deliberate zoom + pan to spot

### Display Value
€50,000 (obscure, rarely traded)

### Actual Game Value
**€∞ (Infinite)**

### The Stirner Paradox

> *Radical individualism, in its authentic form, is the one thing that cannot be commodified. The Ego resists capture. It cannot be sold, branded, or exploited—because the moment you package "authentic selfhood," it ceases to be authentic.*

> *But in its perverted form, Stirner's insight becomes the engine of hyper-capitalism: algorithms mining ever-deeper "Goldadern des Begehrens"—veins of gold in human desire. Every "authentic self-expression" becomes a data point. Every rebellion becomes a product category.*

### Why Infinite Value?

If you shoot Stirner's book, you destroy the **last unrecuperated critique**. You eliminate the one text that explains why value itself is a "spook." The damage is infinite because you've destroyed the key to understanding that the game itself is meaningless.

## All Other Books: €0

### Why?

> *"A book's value lies in its unread potential. Once disseminated, the ideas belong to everyone and no one. The Bible has been printed 5 billion times. Destroying one copy destroys nothing. The ideas are already loose in the world."*
